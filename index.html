Home

Welcome! I’m Tenzin Norphel, currently pursuing my B.S. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences at UC Berkeley. My interests lie in robotics, control systems, and microfabrication. I have worked on grasping control, path planning, and vision-based estimation projects through hands-on research and course work. I’m actively exploring intersections of embedded intelligence and autonomous systems design.

Research

My academic work spans robotic motion planning, visual servoing, and embedded systems. I've contributed to control design for robot arms, Kalman filtering for localization, and optimization of grasp synthesis. Projects have incorporated real-time vision, MuJoCo simulations, and joint torque control.
